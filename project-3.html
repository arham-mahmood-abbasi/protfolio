Project Overview

WhenWe is an online platform that invites people to contribute to a memory book about a person in theirs, and their lives. These invitations may be to someone who has shared a lot, or a little amount of time in the recipient’s life, but is still important to them.

As Senior Developer and Performance Optimization Lead for the WhenWe project, my primary focus was on drastically improving application performance and user experience.

One of my most impactful contributions was reducing the web application's render time from an unacceptable 1 minute to a mere 2 seconds. I achieved this through meticulous profiling and analysis, leveraging SvelteKit with server-side rendering (SSR) to send fully formed HTML to the client for dramatically improved perceived load times and SEO. Beyond SSR, I fine-tuned PostgreSQL queries by rewriting inefficient ones, adding strategic indexes, optimizing joins, and ensuring lean, fast database interactions. This achievement was pivotal in improving user satisfaction and overall application usability.

My work also involved tackling highly complex UI/UX challenges, particularly designing intricate PDF styling for "Memory Books." This demanded pixel-perfect precision to ensure exact replication across various devices and print formats, involving custom CSS and specialized PDF generation. Beyond visual design, I integrated MongoDB for advanced analytics, chosen for its flexibility and scalability to process large volumes of unstructured analytical data efficiently. I designed data models to capture user interactions and performance metrics. Furthermore, I significantly optimized front-end performance beyond just render time, implementing lazy loading, code splitting, and optimizing asset delivery to contribute to a snappier, more fluid user interface.

A fascinating aspect of my role was contributing to the development of a Django-powered chatbot. I was involved in building several versions, focusing on enhancing user interaction and providing more intelligent support. This included working on backend logic to process natural language input, integrate with various data sources, and generate relevant responses, exploring different approaches to conversational flow and intent recognition.

The WhenWe project was a significant growth experience in terms of technical adaptability. The transition to SvelteKit and server-side rendering was entirely new territory for me, yet I rapidly assimilated these complex technologies through dedicated self-study and team guidance. Successfully implementing SvelteKit's SSR capabilities and integrating it seamlessly into the existing Django backend showcased my ability to quickly master novel frameworks and apply them effectively to solve real-world performance challenges.
